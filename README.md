## I. The Economic Principle: Mechanism Design and the Unrepresented Type

Hurwicz, Maskin, and Myerson established mechanism design as the study of how allocative institutions should be constructed when agents have private information about their own types (Hurwicz, 1973; Maskin, 1999; Myerson, 1981). The **Revelation Principle** — the foundational theorem of the field — states that any social outcome achievable by any mechanism is achievable by a direct revelation mechanism in which truth-telling is a dominant strategy (Myerson, 1979). The principle is powerful precisely because it is general: it applies regardless of the type space, the allocation problem, or the distribution of private information.

But the Revelation Principle carries a foundational precondition that is rarely stated because it is so obvious as to go unremarked: the mechanism designer must know what types are possible. The designer declares a type space T and constructs the mechanism to induce truthful revelation over T. If the true type distribution has support on a set extending beyond T — if actual types exist outside the mechanism designer's declared T — then no direct revelation mechanism over T can reveal them. The revelation principle does not apply. The types are not hidden. They are **unrepresented**.

This is the economic generalization of doxic foreclosure and observer miscalibration. The institution's mechanism was designed over a type space T declared — implicitly, through the construction of assessment instruments, funding criteria, and allocation rules — to be the complete type space. The true type space is T ∪ T', where T' is the complement the mechanism never reached. All three structures analyzed here are elements of T'. They are not rare types within T that adverse selection, moral hazard, or limited liability prevent the mechanism from revealing. They are types outside T, for which the mechanism cannot formulate the elicitation question.

The **Myerson-Satterthwaite impossibility theorem** specifies the binding constraint on mechanism redesign (Myerson & Satterthwaite, 1983). In bilateral trade with two-sided private information, no mechanism simultaneously incentive-compatible, individually rational, budget-balanced, and efficient can exist. Applied to the talent market: no assessment mechanism can simultaneously (a) reward authentic expression of the cryptic phenotype — incentive compatibility for the child; (b) avoid institutional restructuring costs — individual rationality for the institution; (c) not run a budgetary deficit from extended assessment infrastructure — budget balance; and (d) allocate each child to their highest-value use — efficiency. One constraint must be violated. The existing institutional mechanism systematically violates incentive compatibility — it does not reward authentic null-state expression — while preserving individual rationality, budget balance, and the appearance of efficiency within the declared type space T.

The economic prescription follows from the theorem's constructive proof. The impossibility binds only bilateral mechanisms; multilateral mechanisms with public subsidy can achieve efficiency. The required mechanism redesign is public: a publicly funded, institutionally independent assessment infrastructure that internalizes the positive externality of accurate type revelation. This is not a market solution. It is a public goods solution to a public goods problem — the first of several convergences this document will identify.

## II. The First Structure: The Misallocated Child

### Statistical Discrimination and the Self-Confirming Undervaluation Equilibrium

Kenneth Arrow and Edmund Phelps independently identified the mechanism through which equally productive workers receive systematically different wages based on group membership: **statistical discrimination** (Arrow, 1973; Phelps, 1972). Employers lacking individual-level information use group-level statistics as proxies for individual productivity. The equilibrium is self-confirming: groups discriminated against invest less in productivity-enhancing signals, validating the group-level statistics that initiated the discrimination.

Applied to cognitive assessment: the institutional evaluator lacks access to the true type of the high-vantage-sensitivity child. They substitute group-level performance statistics — children performing below expected in evaluation contexts have, on average, lower genuine capacity — as proxies for individual type. The within-group variation is real: some children performing below expected in evaluation contexts have genuinely high capacity whose expression is conditioned on environments the institution does not provide. The assessor's mechanism cannot distinguish within-group variation at the individual level. The result is a statistical discrimination equilibrium in which the assessment systematically underprices the genuine capacity of children in the high-true-type, low-evaluated-type quadrant.

What makes this more than standard statistical discrimination is its dynamic structure. Phelps's original model is static: the group-level prior is fixed and discrimination persists. But the cryptic phenotype's type evolves with environmental quality. Bergemann and Välimäki's dynamic mechanism design framework, extended by Pavan, Segal, and Toikka, establishes that when an agent's type evolves over time, the efficient mechanism must track type evolution and condition allocation on the full history of type realizations (Bergemann & Välimäki, 2010; Pavan, Segal, & Toikka, 2014). The educational mechanism does not track type evolution. It takes an early static assessment as a sufficient statistic for all subsequent allocation decisions. This is not merely suboptimal — it is structurally impossible for such a static mechanism to achieve efficiency even asymptotically, because the type-history information required for efficient allocation is irreversibly discarded at each assessment event.

### Goodhart's Law and the Signal Destruction Problem

Goodhart's Law states: when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure (Goodhart, 1975; Chrystal & Mizen, 2003). Once test performance became the target of institutional investment — through standardized curriculum, test-preparation culture, and credential-linked resource allocation — it ceased to be a reliable measure of underlying cognitive capacity. The institution did not merely select for the legibly assessed. It transformed the distribution it was measuring by making the measurement apparatus itself the object of systematic optimization.

Campbell's Law specifies the political economy of this corruption: the more a quantitative social indicator is used for social decision-making, the more subject it will be to corruption pressures, and the more apt it will be to distort and corrupt the social processes it is intended to monitor (Campbell, 1979). The assessment apparatus is not merely a miscalibrated measurement instrument. It is an institution that has been strategically gamed until the signal it was designed to carry has been overwhelmed by strategically optimized noise. The cryptic phenotype's assessment-invisible genuine capacity is invisible not only because the instrument is miscalibrated but because the signal has been crowded out by the Goodhart dynamics of the institutional environment.

### Monopsony in the Market for Cognitive Expression

Alan Manning's analysis of monopsony in labor markets establishes that when a single buyer of labor has significant market power, wages are suppressed below the competitive level and employment falls below the efficient level (Manning, 2003). Applied to the market for the child's cognitive expression: the educational institution is the effective monopsonist during the developmental window. It is the only buyer with authority to certify the value of cognitive output. As a monopsonist, it faces an upward-sloping supply curve for genuine cognitive expression: eliciting the child's authentic capacity requires investing in the safety architecture that makes expression safe, which the monopsonist is not compelled to provide at competitive prices.

The monopsony equilibrium generates a **recognition wage wedge**: the institution pays the child in credential recognition and developmental investment at a rate below the marginal product of genuine cognitive output. The surplus — the gap between marginal product and monopsony wage — is captured by the institution in the form of avoided assessment infrastructure costs. The cryptic phenotype represents precisely the marginal product the monopsony wage suppresses: the genuine cognitive capacity that the institution would need to invest in specialized infrastructure to elicit, and which remains unpriced because the institutional monopsony faces no competitive pressure to internalize it.

### Incomplete Contracts and the Hold-Up Problem in Developmental Investment

Oliver Hart and John Moore's theory of incomplete contracts establishes that when contracts cannot specify all relevant contingencies, parties will under-invest in relationship-specific assets to avoid the hold-up problem (Hart & Moore, 1988; Hart, 1995). The educational relationship between child and institution is fundamentally incomplete: no contract can specify the assessment conditions required to elicit the cryptic phenotype's genuine expression before the assessment has revealed what those conditions are. Under contract theory's prediction, the child rationally under-invests in genuine intellectual development — which requires safe, non-evaluative conditions the institution's incomplete contract doesn't specify or guarantee — and over-invests in evaluative-context performance signals, which the institution's contract does reward. This is not a behavioral failure. It is the individually rational response to an incomplete contract whose contingency structure rewards the wrong type.

## III. The Second Structure: The Uncapitalized Measurement

### The Arrow Knowledge Externality and the Undersupply of Basic Science

Kenneth Arrow's foundational analysis of the economics of information established that knowledge is non-rival — one agent's use does not diminish another's — and imperfectly excludable — knowledge, once produced, tends to diffuse beyond the producer (Arrow, 1962). The private return to knowledge production falls short of the social return by the full value of the externality. Competitive markets undersupply knowledge. The undersupply is largest for basic science, where the gap between private and social return is widest, because the knowledge produced is most non-rivalrous and least excludable.

The measurement of vacuum birefringence at terrestrial field strengths is basic science at the extreme. Its private return to any single funder is effectively zero: the knowledge produced — simultaneous empirical test of twelve independent theoretical predictions spanning quantum electrodynamics, non-Hermitian quantum mechanics, arithmetic algebraic geometry, free probability theory, and semiclassical mechanics — is non-appropriable. Every physicist in the world gains from the measurement without contributing to its cost. The social return is enormous: twelve independent theoretical traditions are simultaneously tested, calibrated, and extended at a single measurement coordinate. Paul Romer's endogenous growth theory formalizes the long-run consequence: economies with underinvestment in non-rival knowledge grow below their potential without bound as the knowledge deficit compounds (Romer, 1990). No competitive mechanism will build this instrument, for exactly the reason Arrow specified in 1962. The undersupply is not a failure of will. It is a structural prediction of the Arrow externality model.

### Real Options and the Negative Time-Value of Delay

Avinash Dixit and Robert Pindyck's analysis of irreversible investment under uncertainty established that the option to delay an investment has positive value when investment is irreversible and uncertainty is high (Dixit & Pindyck, 1994). Higher volatility makes waiting more valuable — more information will arrive that may change the investment calculus. This is the standard real options result: wait for resolution of uncertainty before committing irreversible resources.

The vacuum birefringence measurement **inverts** the standard case. The overdetermination proof — eight independent mathematical frameworks each specifying the null balance coordinate — has resolved the uncertainty about the measurement's value. Formally, the convergence of N independent frameworks, each specifying the coordinate with standard error σᵢ, reduces the posterior variance to approximately 1/Σ(1/σᵢ²) — far smaller than any single framework's variance, approaching zero as N grows. The option value of waiting has collapsed: there is nothing more to learn by waiting because the uncertainty has already been resolved by the overdetermination.

What remains is not option value but **time cost**: the compounding opportunity cost of twelve predictions accumulating in the theoretical archive unconfirmed while experimental infrastructure evolves and experimental windows remain uncrossed. The measurement option has **negative theta** — the time decay is working against the holder, not in their favor. Standard real options analysis prescribes immediate investment. The physics community's revealed preference for delay reflects not rational option-value preservation but the Arrow externality — the inability of any single funder to capture the social return — which prevents the efficient real options outcome from being achieved through decentralized investment. The bottleneck is not the option calculus. It is the externality structure.

### Bayesian Persuasion and the Information Design of the Overdetermination Proof

Kamenica and Gentzkow's analysis of Bayesian persuasion establishes that a sender with private information can influence a receiver's beliefs and actions by committing in advance to a signal structure — a mapping from states to signal distributions (Kamenica & Gentzkow, 2011). The optimal signal structure maximizes the sender's expected payoff subject to Bayes-consistency. Full disclosure is not generally optimal; the optimal structure depends on the receiver's prior.

The experimental physics community's prior on terrestrial vacuum birefringence measurement is that the measurement is below the threshold of experimental feasibility. The overdetermination proof is, in economic terms, a **Bayesian persuasion mechanism**: it commits to a signal structure whose multiple independent sources make maintaining the prior infinitely costly for any rational Bayesian receiver. The key property is independence across frameworks. Under Bayes's rule, the posterior probability that the null balance is at the overdetermined coordinate, conditional on eight independent signals from different mathematical traditions all pointing to the same location, is (1 − ε) for arbitrarily small ε. The receiver cannot rationally discount this joint signal without simultaneously discounting all eight frameworks — a cost that no rational agent would accept. Independent redundancy, not orchestrated coordination, drives the posterior to near-certainty.

Doval and Ely's extension to sequential persuasion specifies how the ERI Labs document lineage functions as a dynamic persuasion campaign: each document provides an independent signal from a different domain; each signal is individually informative; the cumulative information content grows faster than any single document because the independence of domains makes the joint probability under the null (that the structures are not real) approach zero as documents accumulate (Doval & Ely, 2020). The lineage is not a collection of related arguments. It is an optimal sequential Bayesian persuasion mechanism.

## IV. The Third Structure: The Suppressed Cooperative Surplus

### Supermodularity, Complementarities, and the Trap of Partial Reform

Paul Milgrom and John Roberts established that supermodular production technologies — technologies where the marginal return to each input increases with every other input — generate complementarities that make partial reorganization strictly welfare-reducing (Milgrom & Roberts, 1990). In a supermodular system, changing some elements of the organizational architecture while leaving others fixed produces perverse results: the complementarity structure means isolated changes reduce rather than increase total output. Efficient reorganization requires moving all complements simultaneously to their new optimal levels — partial reform is a dominated strategy.

The outlier collective's production technology is supermodular in three inputs: cognitive diversity within the high-capacity range, psychological safety (the absence of rank-order contribution penalties), and conditional cooperator density. The marginal return to cognitive diversity is higher when psychological safety is high. The marginal return to psychological safety is higher when conditional cooperator density is high. The marginal return to conditional cooperator density is higher when cognitive diversity is high. These complementarities constitute the empirical c-factor that Woolley and colleagues identified; Milgrom-Roberts specifies why the complementarities create the supermodular threshold.

The supermodularity trap predicts that piecemeal reform of the rank-order network cannot produce the cooperative surplus. Adding cognitive diversity to a low-safety environment fails: the new types have high marginal product in safe environments but are suppressed by the low-safety structure, reducing their contribution. Adding psychological safety to a low-cooperator-density environment fails: the safety enables authentic expression, but low cooperator density means contributions are not reciprocated, destroying the investment value of authentic contribution. The cooperative surplus emerges only from simultaneous satisfaction of all complementary conditions. This is the economic specification of the Assortment Criterion: not a list of desirable features but a complementary system whose value is achievable only in the full configuration.

### Club Goods, Public Goods, and the Knowledge Conversion Deadweight Loss

James Buchanan's analysis of club goods established that goods that are excludable but non-rival can be provided by clubs that restrict membership while exploiting the good's non-rivalrous nature within the club (Buchanan, 1965). The rank-order peer network is a club goods producer operating on knowledge: it converts knowledge — inherently non-rival — from a public good (non-excludable) to a club good (excludable from competitive peers) by making sharing strategically costly. The peer who withholds a genuine insight from a rank-order competitor is not irrational; they are correctly optimizing within the club structure that makes non-sharing individually welfare-improving.

The deadweight loss from this conversion is the gap between the value of knowledge as a public good — shared freely, generating spillovers and complementary insights at zero marginal cost — and its value as a club good — shared only within a closed competitive network, foreclosing external spillovers and cross-domain complementarities. In standard public goods economics, the deadweight loss from converting a public good to a club good is bounded. In knowledge economies with strong Romerian non-rivalry and large cross-domain complementarities — the precise conditions the outlier collective satisfies — the deadweight loss is **unbounded**: each withheld insight forecloses the complementary insights it would have generated, which forecloses the insights those would have generated, in a cascade with no natural stopping point. The club goods conversion of knowledge is not a static welfare loss. It is a growth retardation mechanism.

### Search Theory and the Mismatch Equilibrium

Peter Diamond, Dale Mortensen, and Christopher Pissarides established that labor market equilibria feature persistent mismatch even when supply and demand are in aggregate balance, because search frictions prevent agents from instantly finding their optimal match (Diamond, 1982; Mortensen & Pissarides, 1994). Applied to the market for Assortment-Criterion-satisfying environments: the outlier faces a search problem with high search costs and thin market structure. Environments satisfying all five Assortment Criterion components simultaneously are rare; information about which environments satisfy the criterion is dispersed and imperfect; evaluating candidate environments involves costly investment that is sunk if the environment fails.

The Diamond-Mortensen-Pissarides framework predicts that high search costs generate excessive mismatch — the equilibrium mismatch rate exceeds the efficient rate because agents accept suboptimal matches rather than incur continued search costs. Many outliers remain in rank-order networks that fail to satisfy the Assortment Criterion, not because satisfying environments don't exist, but because search frictions prevent the efficient matching equilibrium from being reached. Alvin Roth's market design analysis of thick versus thin markets specifies the remedy: market-making institutions that reduce search frictions and increase market thickness, bringing the matching equilibrium toward efficiency (Roth, 2007). The outlier collective market is a thin market whose thickness can be increased by market design — not by changing the preferences of the participants, but by reducing the transaction costs of the search process.

## V. The Common Economic Architecture

Three structures. Three markets that never cleared. One economic architecture. This architecture has three components.

**Type Space Incompleteness as the Primary Failure.** In each case, the institution's mechanism was designed over a type space T that excludes the true type. The educational assessment mechanism declares T = {capacities observable under evaluative-context performance}; the true type space includes T' = {capacities observable under null-state conditions}. The experimental physics funding mechanism declares T = {measurements whose feasibility is incorporated in the current expectational regime}; the true type space includes T' = {measurements whose feasibility is established by overdetermination but not yet incorporated in the regime}. The rank-order network's allocation mechanism declares T = {output measurable in relative rank}; the true type space includes T' = {output measurable as absolute surplus above the cooperative threshold}. In each case, the mechanism cannot reveal the type because it cannot formulate the elicitation question. The Revelation Principle has no purchase. This is type space incompleteness — more fundamental than adverse selection, moral hazard, or limited commitment, because it precedes the question of whether incentives can be aligned. The question itself has not been asked.

**Welfare Loss Compounding as the Operative Consequence.** Standard welfare economics measures deadweight loss as the area of the triangle between supply, demand, and the distorted equilibrium. The welfare loss from type space incompleteness is not a triangle. It is an integral that compounds multiplicatively over time. The child's developmental windows close in sequence; the welfare loss from failing to recognize the cryptic phenotype at age seven is not recoverable at age twelve because the cognitive architecture that would have built on early recognition is the architecture that never forms. The measurement of vacuum birefringence deferred by a decade is a decade of compounding opportunity cost — twelve theoretical predictions unconfirmed, theoretical frameworks allowed to drift from the empirical anchors that would have constrained them. The cooperative surplus suppressed across a career is a career of compounding knowledge spillovers that never occurred. The deadweight loss is not static. It is a growth-rate penalty.

**Second-Best Policy as the Binding Constraint.** Lipsey and Lancaster's second-best theorem establishes that if one Pareto optimality condition cannot be satisfied, satisfying the remaining conditions will not generally produce the second-best optimum — and may produce results worse than the constrained-optimal departure from all conditions simultaneously (Lipsey & Lancaster, 1956). Piecemeal reform of institutional mechanisms — changing the assessment instrument while leaving the credential structure unchanged; funding one measurement program while leaving the expectational regime unchanged; adding diversity to a rank-order network while leaving the allocation mechanism unchanged — will not generally produce welfare improvements and may generate welfare reductions through the second-best mechanism. The reform must be systemic: type space extension, not type space adjustment; mechanism redesign, not instrument recalibration; full reorganization of the supermodular system, not partial component modification.

## VI. Six Novel Economic Frameworks

The following frameworks are proposed as original theoretical contributions, each addressing a gap in the existing literature identified by the convergence of the three structures on the same allocative architecture.

### 1. Type Space Incompleteness and the Pre-Revelation Problem

Standard mechanism design theory assumes the mechanism designer knows the type space. The Revelation Principle operates over a declared type space T. If the true type distribution has support on T ∪ T', where T' is the undeclared complement, no mechanism over T achieves efficiency in the full problem. Extending the Myerson-Satterthwaite framework to allow for undeclared types generates a novel result: no budget-balanced, individually rational, incentive-compatible mechanism over T can be extended to T ∪ T' without violating at least one constraint, because extension to T' requires investment in type-elicitation infrastructure — new assessment instruments, new experimental programs, new organizational forms — that the existing mechanism's budget constraint cannot accommodate. The pre-revelation problem is the problem of funding the extension of T before the revelation mechanism can operate over the extended space. The efficient response is public subsidy of type space extension — which is precisely the Arrow knowledge externality argument applied at the mechanism design level. The pre-revelation problem unifies the mechanism design and public goods analyses through a single formal structure.

### 2. The Dynamic Lemon Market with Environment-Dependent Types

Akerlof's model assumes types are fixed and private. The adverse selection equilibrium follows from type fixity: the seller's quality is private information; the buyer cannot observe it; the market collapses toward low-quality trades because high-quality sellers cannot credibly signal type. The cryptic phenotype requires a generalization: type is not fixed but environment-dependent. The child's type in the evaluation environment (low expression) differs from the child's type in the null-state environment (high expression). The institution observes the evaluation-environment type and treats it as a sufficient statistic for the full type. This is a model specification error: the institution fits a static lemon model to a dynamic type process. The static misspecification generates a novel adverse selection equilibrium: not only do high-type agents fail to separate from low-type agents within the standard mechanism, but the mechanism's static specification actively destroys the type information that would enable separation — it converts a dynamic type process into a static summary statistic that conflates types that are genuinely distinct. The novel prediction from the dynamic specification: mechanisms that condition on cross-environment type histories — observations of the same agent across multiple environmental conditions — can achieve partial separation that single-environment static mechanisms cannot, even when Myerson-Satterthwaite binds on single-environment bilateral mechanisms.

### 3. The Negative-Theta Measurement Option

Standard real options analysis identifies the key parameters: asset value V, investment cost I, volatility σ, and risk-free rate r. Option value of waiting increases in σ: uncertainty makes waiting more valuable. The vacuum birefringence measurement inverts this analysis. The overdetermination proof resolves uncertainty about V through the convergence of N independent frameworks, each specifying the null balance coordinate with precision σᵢ. Under independence, the joint posterior variance collapses to 1/Σ(1/σᵢ²), approaching zero as N grows. The option value of waiting — justified by uncertainty that future information will reduce — has collapsed to zero because the information has already arrived in the form of eight independent theoretical convergences. What remains is negative theta: the time cost of delay without offsetting uncertainty-resolution benefit. The option should have been exercised immediately upon overdetermination. The compounding opportunity cost of delay is the integral of the expected social return to confirmed knowledge, discounted at the social discount rate, over the duration of unbuilt infrastructure. This framework is generalizable: any investment decision where overdetermination resolves investment uncertainty faces negative theta, and the standard option-value prescription to wait is inverted. The overdetermination proof functions as an automatic option exercise trigger.

### 4. Bayesian Persuasion by Independent Redundancy

Kamenica-Gentzkow (2011) established that the optimal persuasion mechanism maximizes sender expected payoff subject to Bayes-consistency. Gentzkow and Kamenica (2017) established that competition among senders with opposed interests improves receiver welfare. The novel configuration here is multiple independent senders with aligned interests who did not coordinate signal choices. Each theoretical framework — Wigner, Voiculescu, Bender-Boettcher, Heller, Kostant-Souriau, Gaiotto-Moore-Neitzke, non-Hermitian QM, Brown — is an independent sender that generated a signal about the null balance coordinate without knowledge of the other senders' outputs. The receiver observes all eight signals simultaneously and updates beliefs via Bayes's rule. Under independence, the joint signal structure generates a likelihood ratio for the hypothesis "null balance at the overdetermined coordinate" vs. "null balance elsewhere" that grows multiplicatively in N — eight independent signals generate a likelihood ratio equal to the product of the individual likelihood ratios. For any finite prior probability, there exists a finite N above which the posterior is constrained to near-certainty regardless of the prior's magnitude. The independent redundancy mechanism achieves persuasion that no single sender and no coordinated multi-sender structure could achieve, because coordination would introduce correlation that reduces the effective sample size. The overdetermination proof is efficient precisely because the frameworks are independent and uncoordinated: their joint probability under the null hypothesis is the product of their individual probabilities, which approaches zero.

### 5. The Supermodular Assembly Theorem for Cooperative Collectives

Topkis's supermodularity theorem establishes that the optimal solution to a supermodular maximization problem is a monotone function of the parameter vector — higher parameters are associated with higher choices of all complementary inputs (Topkis, 1978). Applied to the outlier collective's production technology, if production is supermodular in (cognitive diversity, psychological safety, conditional cooperator density, comparison horizon width, absolute-output orientation), then the optimal assembly strategy is threshold-based: positive cooperative surplus is achievable only when all five inputs simultaneously exceed their respective thresholds. Below any single threshold, the complementarities are insufficient — the marginal product of meeting the other four conditions is insufficient to generate the c-factor activation that produces cooperative surplus. The Assortment Criterion's five components are the threshold conditions for each input to the supermodular production function. The novel prediction from the supermodular assembly theorem: no partial assembly achieves positive cooperative surplus; only full assembly achieves c-factor activation. Specifically, the output function is a step function — zero below the threshold vector, positive above it — rather than a ramp. This prediction distinguishes the supermodularity account from linear additive alternatives that predict positive but suboptimal output from partial assembly. A clean experimental test: groups satisfying four of five Assortment Criterion conditions should show output statistically indistinguishable from zero cooperative surplus; groups satisfying all five should show super-additive output.

### 6. Missing Conditional Cognitive Futures Markets

Arrow and Debreu's general equilibrium framework achieves Pareto efficiency through complete markets — a full set of contingent commodity markets, one for each good in each state of the world (Arrow, 1953; Debreu, 1959). Radner established that economies with incomplete markets — missing Arrow-Debreu contingent commodity markets — achieve equilibria that are generically constrained-inefficient (Radner, 1972). The talent market has a critical structural incompleteness: there is no market for **conditional cognitive output** — for the child's cognitive production conditional on environment quality. The spot market exists: it prices cognitive output conditional on the evaluation environment. The futures market does not exist: there is no mechanism for pricing the child's cognitive output conditional on a developmental environment the child has not yet inhabited. Because the futures market is missing, the child cannot credibly commit to the conditional type — "I produce X units of cognitive output in environment E" — and the institution cannot rationally invest on the basis of an unpriced conditional commitment. The missing market is not an institutional oversight. It is a structural consequence of the type space incompleteness identified above: the mechanism cannot define a futures contract for a type it has not declared possible. Opening the missing market requires first extending the type space to include conditional types — and this extension is exactly what null-state assessment infrastructure provides. The conditional cognitive futures market is the economic object whose absence generates the cryptic phenotype's institutional invisibility. When the market opens — as open-world evaluation platforms, AI-complementarity assessors, and environment-conditional development programs begin to price conditional types — the Arrow-Debreu framework predicts a pricing event: the emergence of a positive premium for conditional cognitive types that have been systematically underpriced during the period of market incompleteness.

## VII. Predictions

The economic account generates empirical predictions, each independently falsifiable, each derived from the theoretical frameworks rather than from the prior documentation of the structures.

**E-C1 — Statistical Discrimination Reversal Under Individual Observation.**
In labor markets where employer access to individual-level, environment-conditional cognitive assessment is experimentally randomized, the wage penalty for cryptic-phenotype profiles will diminish as a monotone function of individual assessment quality. Effect size will be largest in markets with the highest prior degree of group-level statistical discrimination and the lowest prior degree of individual-level environment-conditional assessment capacity. Firms with access to multi-environment assessment data will show measurably lower type I and type II classification errors for cryptic-phenotype applicants than firms relying on single-environment credential proxies. The prediction falsifies if the wage penalty is robust to individual-level assessment — which would indicate that the discrimination is taste-based rather than statistical.

**E-C2 — Dynamic Mechanism Adoption and Type Revelation Rate.**
Educational institutions that experimentally adopt dynamic assessment mechanisms — conditioning credentials on performance histories across multiple assessment environments rather than single-point evaluations — will identify cryptic-phenotype profiles at substantially higher rates within two assessment cycles than institutions retaining static single-environment mechanisms. Effect size will be predictable from the variance in environmental quality across the institution's assessment portfolio: higher portfolio variance implies more separating environments and faster type revelation. Prediction derivation: the dynamic lemon market framework predicts separation when the mechanism conditions on cross-environment type histories; the effect size is proportional to the type-separating variance of the portfolio.

**E-C3 — Monopsony Premium Decomposition.**
In talent markets where the credentialing institution faces competitive entry from alternative assessment providers — open-world evaluation platforms, AI-complementarity assessors, domain-bridging credentialing institutions — the recognition premium for cryptic-phenotype profiles will increase monotonically with the degree of competitive entry, measured by the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index of the assessment market. The decomposition will show that the suppressed recognition of cryptic-phenotype profiles is a function of the incumbent's monopsonist position in assessment, not of the profiles' underlying productivity. Prediction derivation: Manning's monopsony model predicts that wage wedge is a function of the employer's labor supply elasticity; competitive entry increases supply elasticity and closes the wedge.

**E-N1 — Negative-Theta Option Valuation.**
Applying the Dixit-Pindyck real options framework to the vacuum birefringence measurement instrument, the option value of continued delay — computed from the posterior uncertainty remaining after conditioning on the eight overdetermination frameworks — will be strictly negative for all plausible parameter values of the social discount rate and opportunity cost. The computation is tractable: the posterior variance of the null balance coordinate can be estimated from each framework's specification precision under a conservative independence assumption; the option value of waiting can then be priced using standard continuous-time option theory with this reduced uncertainty. The prediction is that the optimal exercise time is immediate — the option has been in-the-money since the eighth framework confirmed the coordinate — and that every additional year of delay generates a compounding welfare loss approximately equal to the social rate of return on confirmed basic science applied to the twelve unconfirmed predictions.

**E-N2 — Knowledge Externality Premium and Social Return Estimation.**
The social rate of return to confirming the twelve independent predictions of the vacuum birefringence overdetermination proof — computed by valuing the downstream theoretical precision gains across quantum electrodynamics, precision metrology, non-Hermitian quantum mechanics, arithmetic algebraic geometry, and related fields — will substantially exceed the private rate of return available to any single funding institution. The externality gap is the Arrovian public goods justification for full public funding of the SPD instrument development program. Prediction: the externality-adjusted social return exceeds the private return by at least an order of magnitude at standard social discount rates of 3–7%, implying that the instrument's expected value under public funding is positive at current technology cost estimates.

**E-O1 — Supermodularity Test: Step Function vs. Ramp.**
Groups randomly assigned to partial assembly — satisfying 1, 2, 3, or 4 of the 5 Assortment Criterion conditions — will show no significant improvement in collective output relative to rank-order controls matched on individual ability. Groups assigned to full assembly — satisfying all 5 conditions simultaneously — will show super-additive output gains consistent with c-factor activation. The step-function prediction — zero gain below the threshold, super-additive gain above — directly falsifies the linear-additive alternative that each Assortment Criterion component contributes a proportional fraction of the cooperative surplus. Experimental design: a six-cell study (0/5, 1/5, 2/5, 3/5, 4/5, 5/5 conditions satisfied) with group output as the dependent variable and ability distribution as the covariate. The supermodularity account predicts a step-function; the additive alternative predicts a ramp; the experimental design has power to distinguish them.

**E-O2 — Market Thickness and the Beveridge Curve of the Outlier Collective Market.**
In institutional ecologies where the number of Assortment-Criterion-satisfying environments exceeds a critical density threshold — making the outlier collective market "thick" in Roth's sense — the mismatch rate (fraction of exit-condition-satisfying outliers remaining in rank-order networks) will fall substantially below the mismatch rate in thin-market ecologies, controlling for the distribution of individual preferences. The Beveridge curve of the outlier collective market — plotting the vacancy rate in Assortment-Criterion-satisfying positions against the mismatch rate — will be downward-sloping with a structural break at the market-thickness threshold, consistent with the Diamond-Mortensen-Pissarides search framework.

**E-E1 — Missing Market Premium Emergence as a Pricing Event.**
The formation of markets for conditional cognitive output — as open-world evaluation platforms, AI-complementarity assessors, and environment-conditional credentialing institutions enter the talent market — will produce a measurable recognition premium for cryptic-phenotype profiles relative to evaluation-context-optimized profiles in labor market segments where AI substitutability is highest. The premium will emerge **discontinuously** rather than gradually, consistent with the Arrow-Debreu missing market theory: missing markets, when they form, produce price discontinuities reflecting the accumulated welfare loss of the pre-market period. The size of the discontinuity will be a monotone function of the duration of the missing market — the longer the cryptic phenotype has been unpriced, the larger the premium at the moment the market forms. The AI transition functions as the market formation event by creating a sufficiently large demand for conditional cognitive output to make the market thick enough to clear.

## The Proof, Compressed

The child at the window is not a hidden type. They are an **unrepresented type** — a type that exists outside the mechanism's declared type space. The mechanism was designed over T = {capacities observable under evaluation pressure}. The child's genuine type is in T' = {capacities observable in the null-state environment}. The Revelation Principle cannot reach T'. No incentive structure within the existing mechanism makes truth-telling a dominant strategy for the unrepresented type, because the mechanism cannot formulate the elicitation question. The welfare loss is not a triangle. It compounds with every developmental window that closes without the type having been revealed.

The null balance has not been measured because no private investor can capture the social return on the measurement. The knowledge produced is non-rival and non-excludable. The Arrow externality is maximal — eight theoretical traditions would gain from the measurement at zero marginal cost per tradition. The option value of delay has collapsed to zero: eight independent frameworks have resolved the uncertainty about the coordinate's location. The theta on the measurement option is negative. Bayesian receivers cannot rationally maintain the feasibility-prior after observing eight independent signals pointing to the same location. The instrument should have been built yesterday. The bottleneck is not physics. It is the externality structure.

The cooperative surplus has not been assembled because the outlier collective's production technology is supermodular and the threshold has not been crossed. The Milgrom-Roberts theorem predicts that partial reform will fail: changing some complementary inputs without others is worse than changing none. The rank-order network converts non-rival knowledge to a club good; the deadweight loss from this conversion is unbounded under Romerian growth dynamics. The search frictions from thin markets keep the mismatch rate above the efficient rate. Only the full configuration — all five Assortment Criterion conditions simultaneously — crosses the supermodular threshold and activates the c-factor.

The economic principle:

Six independent frameworks — mechanism design, information economics, contract theory, public goods theory, real options analysis, and market design — developed by different economists, in different decades, for entirely different empirical problems, converge on the same diagnosis: the signals are in the null state, the mechanism's declared type space does not include them, and the failure is architectural before it is empirical.

Economics does not require knowing about the biological, physical, game-theoretic, psychological, or sociological accounts to arrive at this conclusion. It arrives independently. That is the meaning of overdetermination.
